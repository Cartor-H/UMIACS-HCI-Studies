What Kind of News Reader Are You?

Everyone reads the news a little differently. What’s your style?

This page reveals your News Reader Profile based on the takeaways you’ve written. The chart below highlights five unique news reader styles and shows how closely you align with each one in your Initial and Final Takeaways. Styles that extend farther from the center mean that you align more with them.

The five news reader styles are:
  • The Bridge Builder: “I bridge and weigh different viewpoints to form a balanced perspective.”
  • The Fact Seeker: “I identify and collect key facts and background details to ground my understanding.”
  • The Life Weaver: “I connect the news to my own life and experience to make it relevant and relatable.”
  • The Ripple Tracker: “I trace how one thing leads to another to explain causes, outcomes, and consequences.”
