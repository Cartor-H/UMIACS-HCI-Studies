What Types of Questions Do You Ask?

Everyone brings different curiosities to their news reading. What kinds of questions do you like to ask?

This page shows a chart that breaks down the questions you've asked into four types. A higher bar indicates that you have asked more questions of that type during news reading.

  • Article Viewpoints: You stayed grounded, asking about the different perspectives presented in the news article.
  • Relevant Facts: You focused on concrete facts, asking for clarification or elaboration on key details.
  • Broader Implications: You zoomed out, asking about the bigger picture or larger impact of the news.
  • Personal Relevance: You made it personal, asking how the news connects to your own life, experiences, or actions.

How Does Your Thinking Flow?

Everyone’s mind moves through thoughts and ideas in its own way. How does your thinking flow as you read the news?

This page turns your conversations with the chatbot into a colorful map of your thinking flow. The large chart shows your overall flow – how you moved from one question type to another. The smaller charts zoom in on specific question types.

In these charts, you can follow the ribbons to see:
  • how you transitioned from one question type to another (indicated by the two ends of the same ribbon),
  • how often you made that transition (indicated by the ribbon’s width),
  • whether the transition was initiated by you (indicated by a darker-colored ribbon) or by the chatbot (indicated by a lighter-colored ribbon).

Which Character Best Represents You?

Everyone reads the news a little differently. What kind of news reader are you?

In this chart, there are five distinct characters that describe how people draw takeaways from the news they have read. Characters that extend more distant from the center indicate stronger alignment with you. The figure in grey represents your alignment with each character before leveraging the chatbot for news reading, while the figure in red represents your updated alignment after interacting with the chatbot as your thinking partner.

The five news reader characters are:
  • The Bridge Builder: “I bridge and weigh different viewpoints to form a balanced perspective.”
  • The Fact Seeker: “I identify and collect key facts and background details to ground my understanding.”
  • The Life Weaver: “I connect the news to my own life and experience to make it relevant and relatable.”
  • The Ripple Tracker: “I trace how one thing leads to another to explain causes, outcomes, and consequences.”
  • The Horizon Watcher: “I assess the big picture and generate my judgments about what matters, what’s going well, or what could become a concern.”
